Rob's Guide to Alaska

As a contrast to the usual pointless content on my web site, I decided to write an article that might actually be useful to someone maybe. So I present to you, Rob's Guide to Alaska! In this article, I will touch on the places, people, climate, exports, and attractions that you may or may not find in Alaska.

The Places

As far as I can discern, there are only 4 cities of Alaska, all with a population of 15+. Though the cities' names escape me, I can assure you that they were all quite boring and lame. Each town consisted of about 3 houses, a church, and a about 80 bazillion shops that sold a "wide" variety of "souvenirs."
One interesting thing about Alaska is that if you wander out of the confines of civilization, you are immediately halted by huge towering structures that the natives call "mountains." These "mountains" are unique in the sense that they have no doors or windows with which to enter. I suppose this has been taken as a security measure by the people who dwell inside.

The People

For the purposes of this article, I tried to get an interview with one of the Mountain People who supposedly lived in aforementioned "mountains". Unfortunately, as the Mountain People are extremely elusive, all I could get was an interview with one of the natives who had an extensive knowledge of them.

Me: So tell me, sir, when was your first encounter with the elusive Mountain People?

Native Alaskan: What?

Me: So tell me, sir, when was your first encounter with the elusive Mountain People?

Native Alaskan: No, I heard you, I just didn't-

Me: I see, so you are trying to waste my time?

Native Alaskan: Waste your time? But you were-

Me: You're in cahoots with the Mountain People, aren't you?

Native Alaskan: What are you talking-

Me: Aren't you!?

After that, he tried to run away, but I was able to subdue him and put him in a sack. I had written down an itinerary concerning what I was supposed to do after I'd put him in the sack, but I lost it. I also lost the sheet of paper with the location that I left that sack when I got bored of looking for the itinerary.
But enough about the mountain people. After monitoring the goings-on in a typical Alaskan city, I was able to figure out that 98% of the people in Alaska are tourists, and the other 2% are outcasts from the rest of the planet, thus making Alaska a hole of worthlessness that no one should ever go to.*
*Note to people from Alaska: The previous statement was in regard to a different Alaska on a different planet.**
**Note to people that do not live in Alaska: I'm really not.

The Climate

Contrary to popular belief, Alaska is a very cold place. Of course, when I say "very cold", I am grossly underexaggerating. Imagine walking to a freezer buck naked, on Pluto, in a snowstorm. Obviously, it is somewhat impossible for a snowstorm to exist centralized inside of a freezer, but yes, it's that cold. If you are ever looking to get away from it all and are staggeringly unintelligent, Alaska is the place.

The Exports

It seems to me that the chief exports of Alaska are as follows: mangled pieces of wood that "resemble" various animals, shot glasses with comical illustrations, and ice. Now, you're probably wondering what more any person would want from a state, but I contest that this is surprisingly little for a state of Alaska's size. You see, while all the other 49 states are pulling their weight economically, Alaska is selling snow cones and the like global wide and making a gross profit of $6 yearly. My advice to Alaska is to start programs that support more profitable ventures, such as BEF (Burning Eskimos for Fuel) and WAGHLOLKBIF (We Are Going to Hurt Little Kids Because It's Fun).

The Attractions

There are no attractions in Alaska. Sorry.

Well, that concludes my article, and I hope you've learned as much as I have. Though I don't know why I'd learn anything, because I wrote the article from my previous knowledge and fragments of dreams I had. Anyway, I do have one last thing to show you.
